Dataset settings
Max rows used ? If your dataset is very large, training can take hours. Setting a cap (e.g. 2000 rows) speeds things up while still giving meaningful results. The tool picks a random sample of this size. 2,000
Train / test split ? The data is divided in two halves. The first half trains the AI models. The second half ("control") is used by the privacy tests as a fair comparison group — it represents people who were NOT in the training data. 50% / 50%
Attack queries per model ? Each privacy test sends this many probe queries to try to find real people in the fake data. More queries = more thorough test, but slower. 500 is a good balance for research; use 50–100 for quick demos. 500
Privacy limits ? These are the maximum privacy risk scores a model is allowed to have. If a model's risk is above any of these limits, it is marked "Failed" — meaning it is not safe to release. Lower = stricter. 10% is a reasonable research default.
